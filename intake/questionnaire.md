# Client Intake Questionnaire

## Business identity

1. What is the legal business name?
2. What public-facing business name should be used?
3. What locations or service areas may be published?
4. Who owns final approval for client facts, content, assets, and launch decisions?

## Business goals

1. What business result must this engagement support?
2. How will success be measured?
3. What constraints, deadlines, or dependencies apply?
4. What outcomes are explicitly outside scope?

## Audience and positioning

1. Who is the primary audience?
2. What problems does the business solve for that audience?
3. What should distinguish the business from alternatives?
4. Which statements or claims require verification before publication?

## Services or offers

1. Which services or offers should be presented?
2. What outcome does each service provide?
3. Who is each service intended for?
4. Are prices, service areas, limitations, or qualification rules public?

## Website scope

1. Which pages are required?
2. What is the primary action each page should support?
3. What navigation structure is required?
4. Which existing content may be reused?
5. Which content must be created or rewritten?

## Brand and assets

1. Which logos, icons, photographs, documents, or brand files are authoritative?
2. Who owns or licenses each asset?
3. Are there brand standards for typography, color, spacing, tone, or image use?
4. Which assets are approved for public use?

## Contact and conversion paths

1. Which contact information may be published?
2. Where should form submissions be delivered?
3. What fields should forms collect?
4. What consent, privacy, spam-control, or retention requirements apply?
5. What booking, phone, email, or external conversion links are required?

## Domain, deployment, and ownership

1. Who owns the domain and DNS account?
2. What deployment platform will be used?
3. Who owns the deployment account and billing relationship?
4. Who may authorize production deployment or rollback?
5. What post-launch support and update process is required?

## Analytics, privacy, and legal requirements

1. Which analytics or tracking tools are approved?
2. Is a privacy notice, cookie notice, terms page, accessibility statement, or other disclosure required?
3. Is any regulated or sensitive information involved in the website or forms?
4. What records must be retained, and for how long?

## Acceptance and approval

1. What written criteria must be satisfied before launch?
2. Who approves content, design, functionality, and production release?
3. What evidence is required to confirm approval?
4. What issues may remain open at pilot launch?
